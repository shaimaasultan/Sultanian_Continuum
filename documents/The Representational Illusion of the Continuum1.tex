\begin{abstract}
This paper proposes a fundamental re-evaluation of the Continuum Hypothesis (CH) by demonstrating that the perceived cardinality gap between the natural numbers ($\mathbb{N}$) and the real numbers ($\mathbb{R}$) is a byproduct of restrictive mathematical representation. We introduce a series of Complex Natural Identities, specifically $\sqrt{2} = (i+1)/\sqrt{i}$ and $\pi/2 = -i \ln(i)$, to show that irrational and transcendental constants are exactly representable as operational states of $\mathbb{N}$ within the complex field $\mathbb{C}$. We argue that the multiplicity of complex representations for a single real value proves that the Continuum is a redundant, structured system of natural-based logic, collapsing the hierarchy of infinities into a singular, countable framework. We argue that since $1 \equiv 1.0 \equiv 1+0i$, the "Uncountable" is a structured, countable system of natural-based representations, rendering the Continuum a representational illusion.
\end{abstract}
