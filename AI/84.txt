No, there is **no element in the set of Real Numbers (R) that is not in the union of Irrational Numbers (I) and Rational Numbers (Q)**.

By definition, the set of **Real Numbers (R)** is precisely the union of the set of **Rational Numbers (Q)** and the set of **Irrational Numbers (I)**.
