Let's simplify it step-by-step:

1.  **Simplify the first term:**
    The denominator is $x^3 - \frac{1}{x^3}$. Find a common denominator:
    $$x^3 - \frac{1}{x^3} = \frac{x^3 \cdot x^3}{x^3} - \frac{1}{x^3} = \frac{x^6 - 1}{x^3}$$
    So, the first term becomes:
    $$\frac{1}{\frac{x^6 - 1}{x^3}} = \frac{x^3}{x^6 - 1}$$
    (This assumes $x \neq 0$, and $x^6 - 1 \neq 0$, which means $x \neq 1, -1$, and other complex roots of unity).

2.  **Simplify the product of the two middle terms:**
    $$\left(\frac{x^2-1}{y}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{x^2+1}{y}\right) = \frac{(x^2-1)(x^2+1)}{y \cdot y} = \frac{(x^2)^2 - 1^2}{y^2} = \frac{x^4 - 1}{y^2}$$
    (This uses the difference of squares identity: $(a-b)(a+b) = a^2 - b^2$, where $a=x^2$ and $b=1$).

3.  **Combine all the simplified parts:**
    $$\left(\frac{x^3}{x^6 - 1}\right) \cdot \left(\frac{x^4 - 1}{y^2}\right) \cdot y^2$$

4.  **Perform the multiplication:**
    $$\frac{x^3 \cdot (x^4 - 1) \cdot y^2}{(x^6 - 1) \cdot y^2}$$

5.  **Cancel common terms:**
    Assuming $y^2 \neq 0$ (so $y \neq 0$), the $y^2$ terms cancel out:
    $$\frac{x^3 (x^4 - 1)}{x^6 - 1}$$

6.  **Factor the denominator:**
    The denominator $x^6 - 1$ can be factored as a difference of squares $(x^3)^2 - 1^2 = (x^3 - 1)(x^3 + 1)$.
    It can also be factored using the difference of squares on $x^4-1$ and $x^2-1$:
    $x^6 - 1 = (x^2)^3 - 1^3 = (x^2-1)(x^4+x^2+1)$
    And also, $x^4 - 1 = (x^2-1)(x^2+1)$.

    Let's use the factorization $x^6 - 1 = (x^2-1)(x^4+x^2+1)$.
    So the expression becomes:
    $$\frac{x^3 (x^2-1)(x^2+1)}{(x^2-1)(x^4+x^2+1)}$$

7.  **Cancel common factors again:**
    Assuming $x^2 - 1 \neq 0$ (so $x \neq 1, -1$), the $(x^2-1)$ terms cancel out:
    $$\frac{x^3 (x^2+1)}{x^4+x^2+1}$$
