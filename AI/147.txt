You're trying to weave together the Euler product, prime numbers, and the complex nature of the non-trivial zeros. Let's carefully separate these concepts again, as some key distinctions are still being conflated:

1.  **The Euler Product Formula ($\zeta(s) = \prod_{p \text{ prime}} \frac{1}{1-p^{-s}}$):**
    * This formula is for the **Riemann Zeta function itself**, $\zeta(s)$, valid for $\text{Re}(s) > 1$.
    * It shows that the *function* $\zeta(s)$ is intrinsically linked to and "built from" prime numbers. It provides a way to define or calculate $\zeta(s)$ based on primes.
    * **However, the complex numbers that are the *zeros* of the Zeta function are not themselves expressible as an infinite product over prime numbers.** The zeros are the input values of $s$ for which this prime-built function evaluates to zero.

2.  **Non-trivial Zeros vs. Prime Numbers:**
    * The **non-trivial zeros** of $\zeta(s)$ are **complex numbers** ($s = \sigma + it$). For example, $0.5 + i(14.1347\ldots)$. They have a real part and an imaginary part.
    * **Prime numbers** are **integers** (e.g., 2, 3, 5, 7, ...). Integers do not have an imaginary part (their imaginary part is zero).
    * Therefore, **complex numbers are not simply a "visual representation" of prime numbers in the complex plane.** They are different types of numbers entirely. The zeros are where the zeta function (which has a prime connection) outputs zero.

3.  **Riemann's Hypothesis and "Visualization":**
    * Riemann's hypothesis is a **conjecture** stating that these **complex non-trivial zeros** all lie on the line $\text{Re}(s) = 0.5$.
    * When you "visualize" these zeros, you are plotting these specific complex numbers on the complex plane. The hypothesis is about *their* location.

4.  **Trivial Zeros vs. Non-trivial Zeros and Imaginary Parts:**
    * You are correct that the **trivial zeros** ($s = -2, -4, -6, \ldots$) are on the real axis (the x-axis in the complex plane) and thus have an imaginary part of zero.
    * The **non-trivial zeros** (which are the subject of the RH) are complex numbers and **do** have an imaginary part (they are not on the real axis, except possibly at $s=1/2$, though no zeros are there).
    * **Prime numbers, being integers, do not have an imaginary part.** It's crucial to understand that prime numbers are not the complex non-trivial zeros.

In summary:
The **Zeta function itself** is deeply connected to prime numbers. But the **non-trivial zeros of the Zeta function are complex numbers**, not primes or odd numbers. The Riemann Hypothesis is specifically about the location of these complex zeros on the complex plane.
