That's a very insightful question, and it points to a common misunderstanding of what "fixed value" and "irrational" mean in mathematics. There is **no contradiction** between a number having a fixed, unique value and being irrational. In fact, all real numbers, whether rational or irrational, have a fixed, unique value.

Let's break down what each term means:

1.  **"Fixed, unique value"**:
    * This means that $\pi$ represents one, and only one, specific point on the number line. It's not a range of numbers, it's not changing, and it's not ambiguous.
    * For example, just like the number 2 has a fixed, unique value and is a specific point on the number line, $\pi$ also has a fixed, unique value and is a specific point on the number line (approximately 3.14159...).
    * Every number, by definition, has a fixed, unique value.

2.  **"Irrational number"**:
    * This describes the *nature of its decimal representation* and its inability to be written as a simple fraction $p/q$.
    * An irrational number's decimal expansion goes on forever without repeating any pattern (e.g., $3.1415926535...$).
    * It does **not** mean the number is "unfixed" or "changing." It simply means we cannot express it precisely using a finite number of digits or a simple fraction.

**Analogy:**

Imagine a perfectly precise location on a map, say, the center of a specific tree in a forest.
* That tree's center has a **fixed, unique location** in the world. It doesn't move around.
* Now, if you try to describe that location using GPS coordinates, you might find that the coordinates have an infinite, non-repeating decimal string (e.g., $43.5890^\circ$ N, $79.6441^\circ$ W, but extend those decimals infinitely for perfect precision).
* The fact that the decimal representation of its coordinates is infinite and non-repeating doesn't mean the tree is moving or that its location isn't fixed and unique. It just means that its exact description requires an infinite string of numbers.

Similarly, $\pi$ is a **fixed, unique point** on the number line. The fact that we need an infinite, non-repeating decimal expansion to write it out is a characteristic of its *type* (irrational), not an indication that its value is somehow unfixed or variable.
