You're asking to apply the transformation $s \rightarrow s+0.5$ to the modified functional formula you provided:

$$\zeta(s) = 2^{s+0.5} \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \cdot \pi^{s-1} \sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2}\right) \Gamma(1-s) \zeta(1-s)$$

Let's substitute $s$ with $(s+0.5)$ everywhere in this equation:

1.  **Left side:** $\zeta(s)$ becomes $\zeta(s+0.5)$

2.  **First term ($2^{s+0.5}$):**
    $2^{(s+0.5)+0.5} = 2^{s+1}$

3.  **Second term ($\sin(\frac{\pi}{4})$):**
    This is a constant, so it remains $\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)$

4.  **Third term ($\pi^{s-1}$):**
    $\pi^{(s+0.5)-1} = \pi^{s-0.5}$

5.  **Fourth term ($\sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2}\right)$):**
    $\sin\left(\frac{\pi (s+0.5)}{2}\right) = \sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}\right)$

6.  **Fifth term ($\Gamma(1-s)$):**
    $\Gamma(1-(s+0.5)) = \Gamma(1-s-0.5) = \Gamma(0.5-s)$

7.  **Sixth term ($\zeta(1-s)$):**
    $\zeta(1-(s+0.5)) = \zeta(1-s-0.5) = \zeta(0.5-s)$

Putting it all together, the functional formula when you substitute $s$ with $(s+0.5)$ becomes:

$$\zeta(s+0.5) = 2^{s+1} \cdot \sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right) \cdot \pi^{s-0.5} \sin\left(\frac{\pi s}{2} + \frac{\pi}{4}\right) \Gamma(0.5-s) \zeta(0.5-s)$$

This is a new functional relationship that describes the value of $\zeta$ at $s+0.5$ in terms of other functions of $s$. While this is a valid mathematical transformation of the variable, it results in a different function and does not change the fundamental properties or the meaning of the *original* Riemann Zeta functional equation and its connection to the critical line at $\text{Re}(s) = 1/2$.
