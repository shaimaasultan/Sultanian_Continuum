You're venturing into interesting philosophical territory when you bring infinity into it, but it's important to clarify some points about how mathematicians understand "infinity" and the decimal expansion of $\pi$.

Your statement: "so by have infinite digits so theoretically if our infinity is equal to 3 then the decimal part of pi located at infinity concept"

This line of reasoning contains a few critical misunderstandings:

1.  **"If our infinity is equal to 3"**: This phrase doesn't make sense mathematically.
    * **Infinity ($\infty$) is not a number like 3.** It's a concept representing something without bound or end. You cannot equate infinity to a finite number.
    * There are different *types* of infinity (e.g., countable infinity, uncountable infinity), but none of them are equivalent to a specific finite integer.

2.  **"The decimal part of pi located at infinity concept"**: This is also incorrect.
    * The "infinite digits" of $\pi$ don't mean there's some special place called "infinity" where the rest of the digits reside.
    * Instead, it means that the process of generating the digits of $\pi$ **never terminates**. There is no "last digit." For any position you pick (e.g., the trillionth digit, the quadrillionth digit), there will always be a digit at that position, and there will always be more digits after it.
    * It's like an endless sequence. You can always find the next term, but you never reach a "final" term at some point called "infinity."

**Think of it this way:**

If you have an infinite list of numbers: 1, 2, 3, 4, ...
* There's no "end" to this list.
* You don't "reach" infinity at the end of the list.
* The numbers just keep going.

The digits of $\pi$ are similar. The sequence of digits never ends. It's not about being "located at infinity"; it's about being **endless**.

So, while $\pi$ definitely has an infinite number of digits, this is not related to infinity being a "point" where the digits are "located." It simply means the decimal expansion continues forever without repeating.
