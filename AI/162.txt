1.  **Terminating vs. Non-Terminating Decimal Expansions:**

    * Some numbers have a **non-terminating, non-repeating decimal expansion** (e.g., $\pi = 3.14159...$, $\sqrt{2} = 1.41421...$).

    * Similarly, $1 = 1.0 = 1.000...$ (where the "..." indicates an infinite string of zeros).

    This equivalence means that **a single real number can have multiple decimal representations.**
