Here's how it relates to visualizing 4D, and where the analogy has its limits:

1.  **The Concept:**
    A 4D object exists in a space with four spatial dimensions (e.g., $w, x, y, z$). When we want to "draw" it in 3D, we're essentially taking a "slice" or a "projection" of that 4D object into our familiar 3D space.

3.  **Limitations for true 4D visualization:**

Your observation highlights that if you define a function of $N$ variables, but through algebraic simplification it effectively becomes a function of fewer than $N$ variables, then its graph can be visualized in a lower dimension than initially expected, often through an extrusion.
